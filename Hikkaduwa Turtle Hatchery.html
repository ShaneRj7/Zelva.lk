HIKKADUWA TURTLE HATCHERY

About
Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery

On Sri Lanka's southern coast, there is a conservation facility called the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery. In order to safeguard sea turtles and their eggs from predators and poachers, the hatchery was built in 1996. The hatchery has been an important part of Sri Lanka's sea turtle conservation effort since it opened and is a well-liked tourist destination. The hatchery is managed by a group of qualified experts and volunteers that put forth endless effort to safeguard sea turtles and advance conservation initiatives. The facility provides educational events and guided tours to spread awareness of the challenges to sea turtles and the significance of their conservation.The gathering and preservation of sea turtle eggs is one of the hatchery's main duties.The team gathers eggs from Sri Lanka's southern coast beaches and transports them to a hatchery where they are safely nurtured. The young turtles are raised in tanks after hatching until they are strong enough to be released into the ocean. The hatchery acts as a center for damaged turtle rehabilitation in addition to its conservation initiatives. For turtles hurt by fishing nets, boats, or other dangers, the team offers medical attention and rehabilitation services. Overall, the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery is crucial to Sri Lanka's efforts to preserve sea turtles. Its initiatives have aided in preserving these threatened species and increasing knowledge of the value they play in the ecosystem.

Things To Do

There are several things that visitors can do at the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery to support sea turtle conservation efforts and learn more about these amazing creatures.

Observe the sea turtles: Visitors can observe sea turtles up close and learn about their behavior, biology, and habitat. The hatchery has several tanks where visitors can see baby turtles and injured turtles that are being rehabilitated. Release baby turtles: Visitors can participate in the release of baby turtles into the ocean. This is a unique and unforgettable experience that allows visitors to contribute to the conservation efforts of the hatchery. Learn about sea turtle conservation: The hatchery offers guided tours and educational programs that provide information about the different species of sea turtles that live in Sri Lanka, the threats they face, and what individuals can do to help protect them. Donate to the hatchery: The hatchery is a non-profit organization that relies on donations to support its conservation efforts. Visitors can make a donation to support the hatchery's work. Volunteer at the hatchery: The hatchery accepts volunteers who are interested in contributing to its conservation efforts. Volunteers can assist with tasks such as cleaning tanks, feeding turtles, and monitoring the hatchery's beaches. Overall, there are many ways that visitors can contribute to the conservation efforts of the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery.
By observing sea turtles, participating in releases, learning about conservation efforts, donating, or volunteering, visitors can make a positive impact on the conservation of these magnificent creatures.
